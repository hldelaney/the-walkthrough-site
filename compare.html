Where The Walkthrough is genuinely different

Not louder — different. These are the honest wedges, the places where the design choice actually diverges from the field.

Practice is the whole product

The Walkthrough is a dedicated practice engine — the roleplay and the forensic debrief are the product, not a tab. In Revii, roleplay is one of about four features sitting next to a coaching chat, content generation, and an admin assistant. If what you want is reps, you don’t want a suite.

Both sides of the table — and the call that starts them

Seven scenarios across the full deal: cold-call prospecting, buyer consultation, listing presentation, FSBO conversion, expired-listing reactivation, objection handling (multi-offer, low appraisal, inspection), and negotiation (multi-offer, commission carve-out, appraisal gap). RoleplayPros, by contrast, surfaces buyer-objection drills only — no seller side and no prospecting — and runs web-only. The Walkthrough drills the conversation from the first cold call through the buyer consult, the listing appointment, and the close.

iOS-native, in your pocket

The Walkthrough is a native iPhone app. RoleplayPros, Hyperbound, and Second Nature are all web-only — fine at a desk, awkward for the five minutes before a listing appointment. Practice that travels gets done.

Consumer self-serve at $9.99

One transparent price, sign up in the App Store, cancel anytime. Hyperbound and Second Nature are enterprise tools with opaque, quote-only pricing built for sales orgs, not individual agents. And there’s no $749–$2,999/month coaching contract to sign to get the reps.

An honest word on each

The alternatives are real products with real strengths. Here’s when one of them might be the better call than The Walkthrough.

Revii AI

Real estate · iOS + Android · $19/mo

Revii is the closest comparison: a real-estate app, mobile, low-priced, with voice roleplay and the Tom Ferry brand behind it. If you want one app that also writes your newsletters, drafts social posts, and runs admin tasks — and you value the Tom Ferry name and coaching tie-in — Revii is a reasonable pick.

Where The Walkthrough diverges: it’s a focused practice engine rather than a four-in-one suite, and the debrief is built to point at the specific moment you lost the room rather than hand you a score. If reps and feedback are what you’re after, that focus is the difference.

RoleplayPros

Real estate · web · pricing not public (beta)

RoleplayPros is a real-estate-specific, real-time voice tool with a genuine compliance-alert angle (marketed around Nevada law) and a clean practice loop. If you live at a desktop and want buyer-objection drills with live voice, it’s worth a look.

The honest gaps, from its own materials: it surfaces buyer-objection drills only — no listing-presentation, FSBO, expired, seller-side, or cold-call prospecting scenarios — it’s web-only, it’s early (beta, “Founding 100”), and it doesn’t publish a price. The Walkthrough covers both sides of the table plus prospecting, and runs on your phone.

Hyperbound

B2B sales · web · free tier, then sales-led

Hyperbound is a strong, well-reviewed AI roleplay platform — but for B2B sales teams, not residential real estate. Its personas, scorecards, and integrations (Salesforce, HubSpot) are built for an SDR floor. If you’re running B2B sales enablement, it’s excellent.

For a real estate agent, it’s the wrong vertical: no listing, FSBO, or expired scenarios, web-only, and the useful tier is sales-led/enterprise. The Walkthrough is purpose-built for the real estate conversation at a consumer price.

Second Nature

Enterprise sales / L&D · web · quote-only

Second Nature is a mature, highly rated enterprise role-play platform with deep customization, LMS/CRM integrations, manager analytics, and 25+ languages. For a large customer-facing org standardizing training at scale, it’s a serious tool.

It’s also enterprise-first: opaque quote-only pricing reviewers call prohibitive for small teams, web-only, and not tailored to real-estate scenarios. An individual agent isn’t the buyer. The Walkthrough is self-serve, iOS-native, and real-estate-specific.

And the coaching question — Tom Ferry, mentors, roleplay partners

Human coaching · ~$749–$2,999+/mo

Human coaching does things no app can: real accountability, whole-business strategy, someone who knows your market. The Walkthrough isn’t a replacement for a great coach. It fills the gap coaching can’t — on-demand, unlimited reps between sessions, for $9.99 a month and no contract. Many agents will want both; the app is the daily practice, the coach is the strategy.
